Q4.

For a constant collector-emitter voltage of \(8 \mathrm{~V}\), the collector current of a transistor reached to the value of \(6 \mathrm{~mA}\) from \(4 \mathrm{~mA}\), whereas base current changed from \(20 \,\mu \mathrm{A}\) to \(25 \,\mu \mathrm{A}\) value. If transistor is in active state, small signal current gain (current amplification factor) will be :

  1. 240
  2. 400
  3. 0.0025
  4. 200

Answer Key

  1. Q1: D
  2. Q2: 2
  3. Q3: 10
  4. Q4: B

Explanations

  1. Q1: The current gain of a transistor in common emitter configuration is given by:

    \(\beta = \frac{I_C}{I_B}\)

    where \(I_C\) is the collector current and \(I_B\) is the base current.

    In this case, the collector current changes from \(5 \mathrm{~mA}\) to \(16 \mathrm{~mA}\) for the change in base current from \(100~ \mu \mathrm{A}\) to \(200 ~\mu \mathrm{A}\). Therefore, we have:

    \(\Delta I_C = 16 \mathrm{~mA} - 5 \mathrm{~mA} = 11 \mathrm{~mA}\)

    \(\Delta I_B = 200~ \mu \mathrm{A} - 100~ \mu \mathrm{A} = 100~ \mu \mathrm{A}\)

    Therefore, the current gain of the transistor is:

    \(\beta = \frac{\Delta I_C}{\Delta I_B} = \frac{11 \mathrm{~mA}}{100~ \mu \mathrm{A}} = 110\)

    Therefore, the current gain of the transistor is 110.
  2. Q2:

    Power gain \( = {\left[ {{{\Delta {i_C}} \over {\Delta {i_B}}}} \right]^2} \times {{{R_o}} \over {{R_i}}}\)

    \( = {\left[ {{{{{10}^{ - 2}}} \over {{{10}^{ - 4}}}}} \right]^2} \times {2 \over 1}\)

    \( = 2 \times {10^4}\)

    \( \Rightarrow x = 2\)

  3. Q3:

    \({R_B} = {{10 \times {{10}^{ - 3}}} \over {10 \times {{10}^{ - 6}}}}\)

    \( = {10^3}\,\Omega \)

    \(\therefore\) \({A_v} = \left( {{{\Delta {I_C}} \over {\Delta {I_B}}}} \right) \times \left( {{{{R_C}} \over {{R_B}}}} \right)\)

    \( = {{1.5 \times {{10}^{ - 3}}} \over {10 \times {{10}^{ - 6}}}} \times {{5 \times {{10}^3}} \over {1 \times {{10}^3}}}\)

    \( = {{1.5 \times 5} \over {10}} \times (1000)\)

    \( = 750\)

  4. Q4:

    \(\beta = {{\Delta {I_C}} \over {\Delta {I_B}}}\)

    \( = {{(6 - 4) \times {{10}^{ - 3}}} \over {(25 - 20) \times {{10}^{ - 6}}}}\)

    \( = {2 \over 5} \times {10^3}\)

    \( = 400\)

Q5.

An n.p.n transistor with current gain \(\beta=100\) in common emitter configuration is shown in figure. The output voltage of the amplifier will be

  1. 0.1 V
  2. 1.0 V
  3. 10 V
  4. 100 V

Answer Key

  1. Q1: 1
  2. Q2: D
  3. Q3: 0
  4. Q4: 5
  5. Q5: B

Explanations

  1. Q1: Power gain

    \( \begin{aligned} & \Rightarrow \mathrm{A}_{\mathrm{v}} \cdot \mathrm{A}_1=\mathrm{B} \frac{\mathrm{R}_{\mathrm{C}}}{\mathrm{R}_{\mathrm{B}}} \cdot \mathrm{B}=\mathrm{B}^2 \frac{\mathrm{R}_{\mathrm{C}}}{\mathrm{R}_{\mathrm{B}}} \\\\ & =\left(\frac{(20-10) \times 10^{-3}}{(200-100) \times 10^{-6}}\right) \times \frac{1 \times 10^3}{10 \times 10^3}=10^3 \end{aligned} \)

    Hence \(\mathrm{x}=3\)
  2. Q2: In saturation mode \(\mathrm{V}_{\mathrm{CE}}=0\)


    Now

    \( \begin{aligned} & \mathrm{V}_{\mathrm{CC}}-\mathrm{I}_{\mathrm{c}} \mathrm{R}_{\mathrm{c}}=0 ~~~~\text{(from KVL)}\\\\ & \Rightarrow \mathrm{I}_{\mathrm{b}}=\frac{\mathrm{V}_{\mathrm{CC}}}{\mathrm{R}_{\mathrm{C}}}=\frac{1}{1 \times 10^3}=10^{-3} \mathrm{~A} \end{aligned} \)

    Given that

    \( \beta=100=\frac{I_c}{I_b} \)

    \(\begin{aligned} & \Rightarrow \mathrm{I}_b=\frac{\mathrm{I}_c}{100}=\frac{10^{-3}}{100}=10^{-5} \mathrm{~A} \\\\ & \Rightarrow \mathrm{I}_b=10 \mu \mathrm{A}\end{aligned}\)
  3. Q3:

    Voltage gain \( = {{{I_C}{R_0}} \over {{I_B}{R_i}}}\)

    \( = {{(10\,mA)(60\,\Omega )} \over {(200\,\mu A)(200\,\Omega )}}\)

    \(\Rightarrow\) Voltage gain = 15

  4. Q4:

    Vgain = Current gain \(\times\) \({{{R_L}} \over {{R_i}}}\)

    \( = {{\Delta {I_C}} \over {\Delta {I_B}}} \times {{{R_L}} \over {{R_i}}}\)

    \( = {{5 \times {{10}^{ - 3}}} \over {100 \times {{10}^{ - 6}}}} \times {{2 \times {{10}^3}} \over {0.5 \times {{10}^3}}} = {{10} \over {0.5}} \times 10 = 200\)

  5. Q5:

    \({{{V_0}} \over {{V_i}}} = \beta \times \left( {{{{R_C}} \over {{R_B}}}} \right)\)

    \( \Rightarrow {V_0} = 100 \times \left( {{{10} \over 1}} \right) \times {10^{ - 3}}\)

    \( = 1.0\) V
