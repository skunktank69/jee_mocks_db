Explanations

  1. Q1:

    \( B = \operatorname{adj}(\operatorname{adj}(2A)) = \det(2A) \cdot (2A) \)

    Since \( A \) is a \( 3 \times 3 \) matrix with

    \( \det(A) = \frac{1}{2}, \)

    the determinant of \( 2A \) is computed as

    \( \det(2A) = 2^3 \det(A) = 8 \cdot \frac{1}{2} = 4. \)

    Thus,

    \( B = 4 \cdot (2A) = 8A. \)

    Now, compute the determinant and the trace of \( B \):

    Determinant of \( B \):

    \( \det(B) = \det(8A) = 8^3 \det(A) = 512 \cdot \frac{1}{2} = 256. \)

    Trace of \( B \):

    \( \operatorname{trace}(B) = \operatorname{trace}(8A) = 8 \cdot \operatorname{trace}(A) = 8 \cdot 3 = 24. \)

    Finally, adding these results:

    \( \det(B) + \operatorname{trace}(B) = 256 + 24 = 280. \)

  2. Q2:

    \(|A-x I|=0\)

    Roots are \(-\)1 and 3

    Sum of roots \(=\operatorname{tr}(A)=2\)

    Product of roots \(=|\mathrm{A}|=-3\)

    Let \(A=\left[\begin{array}{ll}a & b \\ c & d\end{array}\right]\)

    We have \(\mathrm{a}+\mathrm{d}=2\)

    \(\mathrm{ad}-\mathrm{bc}=-3\)

    \(A^2=\left[\begin{array}{ll}a & b \\ c & d \end{array}\right] \times\left[\begin{array}{ll} a & b \\ c & d \end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{ll} a^2+b c & a b+b d \\ a c+c d & b c+d^2 \end{array}\right]\)

    We need \(a^2+b c+b c+d^2\)

    \(\begin{aligned} & =a^2+2 b c+d^2 \\ & =(a+d)^2-2 a d+2 b c \\ & =4-2(a d-b c) \\ & =4-2(-3) \\ & =4+6 \\ & =10 \end{aligned}\)
