Q1.
Let \(\mathrm{P}\) be a point on the ellipse \(\frac{x^2}{9}+\frac{y^2}{4}=1\). Let the line passing through \(\mathrm{P}\) and parallel to \(y\)-axis meet the circle \(x^2+y^2=9\) at point \(\mathrm{Q}\) such that \(\mathrm{P}\) and \(\mathrm{Q}\) are on the same side of the \(x\)-axis. Then, the eccentricity of the locus of the point \(R\) on \(P Q\) such that \(P R: R Q=4: 3\) as \(P\) moves on the ellipse, is :
  1. \(\frac{13}{21}\)
  2. \(\frac{\sqrt{139}}{23}\)
  3. \(\frac{\sqrt{13}}{7}\)
  4. \(\frac{11}{19}\)
Q2.

The locus of the mid point of the line segment joining the point (4, 3) and the points on the ellipse \({x^2} + 2{y^2} = 4\) is an ellipse with eccentricity :

  1. \[{{\sqrt 3 } \over 2}\]
  2. \[{1 \over {2\sqrt 2 }}\]
  3. \[{1 \over {\sqrt 2 }}\]
  4. \[{1 \over 2}\]

Answer Key

  1. Q1: C
  2. Q2: C

Explanations

  1. Q1:
    \(\begin{aligned} & \mathrm{h}=3 \cos \theta \\\\ & \mathrm{k}=\frac{18}{7} \sin \theta\end{aligned}\)

    \(\begin{aligned} & \therefore \text { locus }=\frac{\mathrm{x}^2}{9}+\frac{49 \mathrm{y}^2}{324}=1 \\\\ & \mathrm{e}=\sqrt{1-\frac{324}{49 \times 9}}=\frac{\sqrt{117}}{21}=\frac{\sqrt{13}}{7}\end{aligned}\)
  2. Q2:

    Let \(P(2\cos \theta ,\,\sqrt 2 \sin \theta )\) be any point on ellipse \({{{x^2}} \over 4} + {{{y^2}} \over 2} = 1\) and Q(4, 3) and let (h, k) be the mid point of PQ

    then \(h = {{2\cos \theta + 4} \over 2},\,k = {{\sqrt 2 \sin \theta + 3} \over 2}\)

    \(\therefore\) \(\cos \theta = h - 2,\,\sin \theta = {{2k - 3} \over {\sqrt 2 }}\)

    \(\therefore\) \({(h - 2)^2} + {\left( {{{2k - 3} \over {\sqrt 2 }}} \right)^2} = 1\)

    \( \Rightarrow {{{{(x - 2)}^2}} \over 1} + {{{{\left( {y - {3 \over 2}} \right)}^2}} \over {{1 \over 2}}} = 1\)

    \(\therefore\) \(e = \sqrt {1 - {1 \over 2}} = {1 \over {\sqrt 2 }}\)
