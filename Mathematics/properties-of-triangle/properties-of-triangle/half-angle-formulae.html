Q1.
If the line \(x=y=z\) intersects the line

\(x \sin A+y \sin B+z \sin C-18=0=x \sin 2 A+y \sin 2 B+z \sin 2 C-9\),

where \(A, B, C\) are the angles of a triangle \(A B C\), then \(80\left(\sin \frac{A}{2} \sin \frac{B}{2} \sin \frac{C}{2}\right)\)

is equal to ______________.

Answer Key

  1. Q1: 18

Explanations

  1. Q1: \( \begin{aligned} &x= y=z=k(\text { let }) \\\\ &\therefore k(\sin A+\sin B+\sin C)=18 \\\\ &\Rightarrow k\left(4 \cos \frac{A}{2} \cdot \cos \frac{B}{2} \cdot \cos \frac{C}{2}\right)=18 \\\\ & k(\sin 2 A+\sin 2 B+\sin 2 C)=9 \\\\ &\Rightarrow k(4 \sin A \cdot \sin B \cdot \sin C)=9 \ldots \text { (ii) } \\\\ & \text {(ii)} /\text {(i)} \\\\ & 8 \sin \frac{A}{2} \cdot \sin \frac{B}{2} \cdot \sin \frac{C}{2}=\frac{9}{18} \\\\ &\Rightarrow 80 \sin \frac{A}{2} \cdot \sin \frac{B}{2} \cdot \sin \frac{C}{2}=5 \end{aligned} \)
