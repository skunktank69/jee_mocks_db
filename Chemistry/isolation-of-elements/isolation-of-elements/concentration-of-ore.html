  3. Q3: Calcination is the process of heating a solid material in the absence of air or with limited air to remove volatile components or to cause thermal decomposition. Among the given reactions:

    (A) \(\mathrm{CaCO}_3 \cdot \mathrm{MgCO}_3 \stackrel{\Delta}{\longrightarrow} \mathrm{CaO}+\mathrm{MgO}+2 \mathrm{CO}_2\) is an example of calcination, where a mixture of calcium carbonate and magnesium carbonate is heated to produce calcium oxide, magnesium oxide, and carbon dioxide.

    (C) \(\mathrm{CaCO}_3 \stackrel{\Delta}{\longrightarrow} \mathrm{CaO}+\mathrm{CO}_2\) is an example of calcination, where calcium carbonate is heated to produce calcium oxide and carbon dioxide.

    (D) \(\mathrm{Fe}_2 \mathrm{O}_3 \cdot \mathrm{xH}_2 \mathrm{O} \stackrel{\Delta}{\longrightarrow} \mathrm{Fe}_2 \mathrm{O}_3+\mathrm{xH}_2 \mathrm{O}\) is an example of calcination, where hydrated iron(III) oxide loses water molecules to form anhydrous iron(III) oxide.

    However, (B) \(2 \mathrm{PbS}+3 \mathrm{O}_2 \stackrel{\Delta}{\longrightarrow} 2 \mathrm{PbO}+2 \mathrm{SO}_2\) is not an example of calcination. This reaction is an example of roasting, a process in which a sulfide ore is heated in the presence of air to convert it into its oxide and release sulfur dioxide gas. Roasting is typically used for metal extraction from sulfide ores.

    So the correct answer is:

    (B) \(2 \mathrm{PbS}+3 \mathrm{O}_2 \stackrel{\Delta}{\longrightarrow} 2 \mathrm{PbO}+2 \mathrm{SO}_2\)
  4. Q4: Option A is the correct answer.

    Statement I : In the metallurgy process, sulphide ore is converted to oxide before reduction.

    This is correct. In metallurgy, it is common to first convert sulphide ores to oxides because it's typically easier to reduce oxides to extract the metal. This process is known as roasting, where the sulphide ore is heated in the presence of air to convert it into an oxide.

    \( 2 \mathrm{ZnS}+3 \mathrm{O}_2 \rightarrow 2 \mathrm{ZnO}+2 \mathrm{SO}_2 \)

    Statement II : Oxide ores in general are easier to reduce.

    This is also correct. Oxides are generally easier to reduce than most other types of compounds. This is because the bond between metal and oxygen in metal oxides is often weaker than the bond between metal and other elements in various types of ores. For example, the C-O bond in CO2 (a product of the reduction of a metal oxide) is weaker and more easily broken than the C-S bond in CS2 (a product of the reduction of a metal sulphide), making CO2 more volatile and easier to remove from the system.

    So, both Statement I and Statement II are correct, making Option A the right choice.
